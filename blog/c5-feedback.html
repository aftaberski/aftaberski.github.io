Let's Talk About Feelings And Feedback

NOVEMBER 30, 2014

Feedback is an iffy thing sometimes. For starters, I'm always game for positive feedback. I mainly just want people to tell me I'm awesome (because, honestly, who doesn't?), so sometimes constructive feedback can come as a bit of a slap in the face if I'm feeling a lil vulnerable at that moment. That part can be not so fun. It can be quite an emotional rollercoaster. Luckily, most feedback is given with the intention of helping you become a better programmer as opposed to cutting you down, so the constructive part definitely doesn't need to be taken so negatively. Being able to recognize my shortcomings has only allowed me to account for them to help me step up my programming game! Here's a glimpse at my emotions as I read some of my peer feedback.

First, there's the anticipation. "Do I reeeeeally want to know what someone else thinks of me?"

"Yeaaaaah, ok I should probably see what they have to say..."

And then you start reading and it's like...

Keep the compliments comin'

But then you read some of the constructive criticism...

And for a second, you feel a little bit like...

But then you think about it for a minute

And you realize maybe this feedback makes a pretty good point

So you take a good hard look at yourself and make some slight adjustments

And at your next pairing session, you realize things are going even better now that you accepted some of this constructive feedback

And finally, there's only one thing left to say: "Thank you very much for both your positive and constructive feedback. You guys are all helping make me a better person and a better pairing partner. THANK YOU THANK YOU THANK YOU!"

In all seriousness, thanks for pairing with me, guys. Working together has helped me learn so much more than I would have learned on my own. Glad we're all in this together
